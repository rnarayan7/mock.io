What is mock?
Mock is a platform by which individuals can connect with one another for the purpose of conducting mock interviews. It provides a method by which people who have already received offers from a specific company, or even seasoned interviewers can source themselves for interviews. As a job seeker, it is the perfect tool for receiving personalized help towards landing one's dream offer.
Who is it for?
Mock was initially built to cater to university students, given that at many schools, students are unable to find the necessary help to prepare for specific interviews. As of now, it continues to serve university students as its primary goal, but the development team is looking to expand its usage to other target groups.

University of Michigan
Mock's first home was at the University of Michigan. While studying there, Roshan identified some of the shortcomings of the College of Engineering's Career Resource Center, specifically its inability to provide personalized interviews for roles in software engineering. Aiming to build a better system to help Michigan students, he created Mock and pitched it first to fellow peers. Some of them were the first ever to use the platform.
UM School of Information
This idea came to reality largely due to the teachings of two classes in the University of Michigan School of Information, Succeeding at Failing: The Art of Entrepeneurship in the Age of Information (SI 360) and Accessible Web Design (SI 339). Various aspects of the product design and business model came from a startup pitch challenge in SI 360 and the design techniques taught in SI 339 aided in developing the first iterations of the product.
